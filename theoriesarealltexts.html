theories are all texts

Actually, the theory — i.e., what we understand and remember — is the ghost of the text, which by definition is just a string of words. (In the same way, the meaning is the ghost of the definition inscribed in a lexicon.)

One can memorize the string of words without understanding it; whether the theory can exist without the words is a harder question to answer.
